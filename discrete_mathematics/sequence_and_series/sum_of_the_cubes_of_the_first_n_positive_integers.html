Proof That The Sum Of The Cubes Of The First n Positive Integers = (n(n+1)/2)^2

Lets evaluate \(\Sigma^n_{i=1} \left[\left(1+i\right)^4 - i ^4\right]\):

\[\Sigma^n_{i=1} \left[\left(1+i\right)^4 - i ^4\right] = \Sigma^n_{i=1}\left[4i^3+6i^2+4i+1\right] = 4 \Sigma^n_{i=1} i^3 + 6 \Sigma^n_{i=1} i^2 + 4 \Sigma^n_{i=1} i+ \Sigma^n_{i=1}1\]

Let \(S\) be what we are trying to proof. We will call the equation below (1):

\[\Sigma^n_{i=1} \left[\left(1+i\right)^4 - i ^4\right] = 4 S + n\left(n+1\right)\left(2n+1\right)+2n \left(n+1\right)+ n \tag{1} \]

Lets expand \(\Sigma^n_{i=1} \left[\left(1+i\right)^4 - i ^4\right]\):

\[\Sigma^n_{i=1} \left[\left(1+i\right)^4 - i ^4\right] = \left(2^4-1^4\right)+\left(3^4-2^4\right)+\left(4^4-3^4\right)+\ldots+\left(\left(n+1\right)^4-n^4\right)\]

Alot of terms cancel out:

\[\Sigma^n_{i=1} \left[\left(1+i\right)^4 - i ^4\right] = \left(\cancel{2^4}-1^4\right)+\left(\cancel{3^4}-\cancel{2^4}\right)+\left(\cancel{4^4}-\cancel{3^4}\right)+\ldots+\left(\left(n+1\right)^4-\cancel{n^4}\right)\]

Simplify:

\[\Sigma^n_{i=1} \left[\left(1+i\right)^4 - i ^4\right] = \left(n+1\right)^4 - 1\]

We will call this equation (2):

\[\Sigma^n_{i=1} \left[\left(1+i\right)^4 - i ^4\right] = n^4 +2 n^3 + n^2\tag{2}\]

Combining (1) and (2):

\[n^4 +2 n^3 + n^2=4 S + n\left(n+1\right)\left(2n+1\right)+2n \left(n+1\right)+ n\]

Simplifying this gives us our answer:

\[S=\frac{n^2{\left(n+1\right)}^2}{4}={\left(\frac{n\left(n+1\right)}{2}\right)}^2\]
