Proof That The Sum Of The Squares Of The First n Positive Integers = n(n+1)(2n+1)/6

Lets evaluate \(\Sigma^n_{i=1} \left[\left(1+i\right)^3 - i ^3\right]\):

\[\Sigma^n_{i=1} \left[\left(1+i\right)^3 - i ^3\right] = \Sigma^n_{i=1}\left[3i^2+3i+1\right] = 3 \Sigma^n_{i=1} i^2+ 3 \Sigma^n_{i=1} i+ \Sigma^n_{i=1}1\]

Let \(S\) be what we are trying to proof. We will call the equation below (1):

\[\tag{1} \Sigma^n_{i=1} \left[\left(1+i\right)^3 - i ^3\right] = 3 S +3\frac{n(n+1)}{2}+ n \]

Lets expand \(\Sigma^n_{i=1} \left[\left(1+i\right)^3 - i ^3\right]\):

\[\Sigma^n_{i=1} \left[\left(1+i\right)^3 - i ^3\right] = \left(2^3-1^3\right)+\left(3^3-2^3\right)+\left(4^3-3^3\right)+\ldots+\left(\left(n+1\right)^3-n^3\right)\]

Alot of terms cancel out:

\[\Sigma^n_{i=1} \left[\left(1+i\right)^3 - i ^3\right] = \left(\cancel{2^3}-1^3\right)+\left(\cancel{3^3}-\cancel{2^3}\right)+\left(\cancel{4^3}-\cancel{3^3}\right)+\ldots+\left(\left(n+1\right)^3-\cancel{n^3}\right)\]

Simplify:

\[\Sigma^n_{i=1} \left[\left(1+i\right)^3 - i ^3\right] = \left(n+1\right)^3 - 1\]

We will call this equation (2):

\[\Sigma^n_{i=1} \left[\left(1+i\right)^3 - i ^3\right] = n^3 +3 n^2 + 3n\tag{2}\]

Combining (1) and (2):

\[n^3 +3 n^2 + 3n=3 S +3\frac{n(n+1)}{2}+ n\]

Simplifying this gives us our answer:

\[S=\frac{n\left(n+1\right)\left(2n+1\right)}{6}\]
